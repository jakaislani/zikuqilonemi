Child Abuse Charge &amp; Criminal Record

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Wednesday, June 26, 2024

David Eason mugshot and child abuse charges have put him in some serious trouble with the law. Get updates on his arrest charges and the case.

David Eason, the husband of former Teen Mom star Jenelle Evans, has been in the spotlight for all the wrong reasons lately.

He has been charged with child abuse in a case involving Jenelle’s 14-year-old son, Jace, who ran away from home multiple times and accused David of assaulting him.

David has also been arrested on other charges, such as violating a protective order and driving with a revoked license.

What is the truth behind David Eason’s mugshot and child abuse allegations? How does his criminal record and arrest history affect his family and his future?

In this article, we will explore these questions and more.

David Eason Mugshot: Why Was He Arrested On Child Abuse Charges?

David Eason’s mugshot went viral after he was arrested on October 15, 2023, on charges of misdemeanor child abuse and injury to a child.

The arrest came after an investigation by the police and child protective services into the case of Jace, Jenelle’s son from a previous relationship, who is David’s stepson.

Jace had run away from David and Jenelle’s home, known as “The Land,” three times in less than two months, claiming that he was afraid of David and that he had physically abused him.

According to TMZ, there is video evidence of an assault involving Jace, which both the cops and CPS had in their possession.

Jace also had visible marks on his neck and arm when the police found him.

Jace is currently staying with his grandmother, Barbara, who raised him for most of his life as Jenelle was deemed an unfit parent.

David Eason’s Criminal Record And Arrest History: What Else Has He Done?

David Eason’s arrest on child abuse charges is not his first brush with the law.

He has a long criminal record and arrest history that dates back to 2008 when he was charged with breaking, theft, possession of drug paraphernalia, and injury to personal property.

Since then, he has been arrested multiple times for various offenses, such as driving under the influence, domestic violence, and violating a protective order.

Likewise, he was also arrested for possession of a firearm by a felon, assault with a deadly weapon, and driving with a revoked license.

He has also been involved in several controversies, such as shooting and killing the family dog after it allegedly bit their young daughter in the face in 2019 (he was not charged for that incident).

His other controversies include posting homophobic and transphobic comments on social media, threatening to kill the president of the United States, and being fired from Teen Mom for his violent behavior.

David faces up to 150 days in jail if convicted of the child abuse charge.

He also risks losing custody of his daughter Ensley, who he shares with Jenelle, as well as his other children from previous relationships.

Jenelle has not yet been charged in relation to the child abuse case, but she could face charges as well.
